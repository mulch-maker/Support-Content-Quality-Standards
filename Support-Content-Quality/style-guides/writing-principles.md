# Support Writing Principles

*Source: https://blockcell.sqprod.co/sites/writers-block/docs/support/support-writing-principles*

## Overview

Block's support content follows six fundamental principles that ensure consistency, clarity, and effectiveness across all customer-facing documentation.

## The Six Core Principles

### 1. One article, one purpose
Each piece of content should address a single task, issue, or decision. This keeps content focused and prevents confusion.

### 2. Be clear, concise, and purposeful
- Use plain language at 6th-8th grade reading level
- Use active voice whenever possible
- Get to the point quickly without unnecessary complexity

### 3. Structure content to guide and empower
- Start with key context that customers need
- Organize information logically
- Use headings, bullet points, and lists to break up text
- Make content scannable

### 4. Write for the customer experience first
- Lead with customer intent and needs
- Respect customers' time
- Design content that enables customer independence
- Focus on what customers want to accomplish

### 5. Design for a global audience
- Write content that can be easily localized
- Avoid idioms, colloquialisms, and cultural references
- Use consistent terminology across all content
- Consider different cultural contexts

### 6. Be consistent across channels
- Maintain the same voice and tone across all content types
- Use standardized templates and formats
- Ensure messaging aligns across different platforms and touchpoints

## Application

These principles should guide all support content creation, from help center articles to in-app messaging, ensuring that customers receive consistent, helpful, and accessible information regardless of how they interact with Block's products and services.
