## Overview

The purpose of this document is to provide a guide regarding CF1 (Salesforce Lightning) Knowledge Articles.

## Guidelines

### Naming Conventions

Use a consistent format for titles to make sure the articles appear when you search. This helps users as well as our team when managing articles.

**Format:** Team/Product - Issue - Sub-Issue or Region (if needed)

**Examples:**
- Cash App Card - Digital Wallet
- Cash App Card Disputes - Mobile Pay Fraud (Reg E)

### Copy-paste from Google

Use the same font throughout your draft document. If you copy/paste into CF1, it will carry over your font and spacing (usually). Text copied from a Google Doc will not be properly formatted in CF1. Images should never be pasted from a Google Doc into CF1.

**To ensure proper formatting:**
- Paste your text into the CF1 field, select it, and click the "remove formatting" button
- Or when you paste into CF1 use the "shift + cmd + V" keys to paste your text without formatting
- You can then reformat your text using the CF1 tools
- Images should be downloaded to your computer, then uploaded to CF1

### Links

#### Linking to other Knowledge articles

If you reference another Knowledge article in your article, you should link to it using Smart Links. This is a more powerful way of linking than hyperlinking the article URL.

⚠️ **Important:** If you don't use Smart Links correctly this negatively impacts our advocates and customers. The link will open in a new tab or window and cause the advocate's Omnichannel to pull extra support cases.

**Steps:**
1. Click the Smart Link Button
2. Search for and select the article in question
3. Once you locate your article, set your link preferences as:
   - **Target:** Same Window (_self)
   - **Salesforce Target:** [leave blank]

**Note:** You can link to Draft CF1 articles and the link will update when the article is published. Select "Draft" in the "Publishing Status"

#### Linking to external (non-CF1) pages

If you need to link to something outside of CF1, like the Newsfeed or Help Center, you will use a normal hyperlink.

**Steps:**
1. Highlight the KA body text and select the link button (or use shortcut cmd+K)
2. This link should open in a New Window(_blank)
3. If you include a link in your article body (not the Related Content section), consider giving your reader a heads up that the link is outside of CF1

#### Header anchors

Learn more about creating section anchors and linking to those in your Knowledge article.

### Structure

Large topics may require multiple articles. In this case, create a landing page article with links to each of the topic articles.

**Word count guidelines:**
- **Goal word count** for each topic article: 4,000 words
- **Max word count:** 20,000 words

Cash Customer Operations aims to help customers in real-time. While we do serve some customers through Email (mostly investigative or specialty teams like Disputes and Investing), Messaging and Voice are our primary Customer Support channels.

**Always list in this order:**
1. Voice
2. Messaging  
3. Email steps

### Word Counts/Character Limits

- **Goal max word count per article:** < 4,000
- **Max total word count:** 10,000
- **CF1 Character limit:** 131,072 (includes links, graphics and formatting characters)
  - *This limit cannot be increased*

### Segmenting Articles

#### Article Density
- Adhere to word count limits. Long, dense articles are hard to follow and can make it harder to help customers
- Reduce word count by sticking to only the essential information and steps – remove unnecessary descriptions
- Use bullets and numbered steps to break up articles
- If the article has multiple paragraphs of more than 3 sentences, re-evaluate the content and re-organize it

#### Headings
- Headings help break information into smaller chunks and draw the reader to specific sections
- All headings are sentence case

#### Density
Cash App has some complex customer service procedures, but we should minimize procedures whenever possible to make knowledge articles shorter and therefore easier and faster to read.

#### Link Consistency
Some links open new tabs. Some open new windows. All links should function consistently. Make sure they are set to open in the Same Window or Tab.

#### Glossary Articles
Separate glossary and Landing Page articles could help CCO teams find what they need faster—especially when dealing with complex issues. Create Glossary articles only when there are a lot of workflow- or feature-specific terms that need to be referenced more than once. Otherwise include terms and definitions within the article itself (tables work well for this).

#### Image Action Consistency
- Make sure visual workflows and screenshots are consistent in size and use the workflow template
- Check the published version of the article to make sure that images aren't stretched or broken
- Images count against character count so make sure you upload and insert them and don't copy + paste from Google Docs

### Channel-specific Workflows

We support customers across multiple channels (Voice, Messaging, Social, and Email for certain case types). Workflows may differ by channel. CF1 has limitations about how we can segment out Knowledge Articles, so use these guidelines to decide how to structure your articles:

#### Create new, channel-specific article or sets of articles when:
**1/4 or more of the workflow is unique to a certain channel**

**Example:** Messaging Advocates assisting customers over chat with Cash Card disputes by directing them through the transaction picker and submitting the IRF in app. In Voice, advocates can fill out the IRF on the phone on behalf of the customer, step by step. These steps and responsibilities are more than different enough to justify separate articles.

#### Create a section within an existing article when:
**Less than 1/4 of the workflow is unique to a certain channel**

**Example:** If four out of the 5 steps to escalate to a manager are the same for Voice as they are for Messaging and other CS Advocates, keep all steps in the same article. Call out channel-specific deviations like this:
- Provide Cash App {Quick Text} to the customer to let them know that their dispute claim is still being reviewed.
- **Voice:** Confirm the customer's email address before sending Cash App {Claim Docs}

**Naming conventions:** place channel name first
- Voice - Cash App Card Disputes - Submitting IRF
- Social - Reporting Scammers

### Formatting Tips

Simplicity is key. In addition to following the Knowledge Article template, look for opportunities to simplify and streamline the information. To make your text easier for Advocates and Agents to scan, use formatting:

- Break longer articles into short paragraphs with different headings and subheadings
- Include table of contents at beginning with jumplinks (anchor links) so users can jump straight to the section that is most relevant to them
- Use bullet points and numbered lists to break up blocks of text and make it more readable
- Use bold, italicized, or highlighted text to draw the reader's attention to key pieces of information

Find more resources in the style guide and [CCO Support Content Style Guide](https://docs.google.com/document/d/185CN7rRvBmyPAo-MGbkj9xgvhIfTnz08Fv5v9LHIzsA/edit?usp=sharing).

### Publishing/Scheduling

- You can schedule knowledge articles to go live on a certain date and time
- You can't cancel a draft scheduled to publish
- If you need to change the date, you will need to either click publish and then change the date, or delete the draft and then republish
- Versions field will show you all published versions and drafts (going back 30 versions - need a report for more than that from CBS)
