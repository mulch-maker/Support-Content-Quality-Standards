# How to Write Internal and Customer Steps

*Source: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1bmdx1pKzwUVmcj9PeYDqmraa_iSYudVJWYPaD1uCYZc/edit*

## Context

How we write internal steps is an extension of our writing principles and values. Using a consistent and clear format helps to establish trust with readers. Our updated approach to writing steps builds on our existing guidance for "lists" and prepares us to move into a modular content future.

## ⏰ When to Use Steps

TKO will shift how we write advocate and customer steps, however, the following guidance will help determine when to use a set of steps in the meantime.

**If ANY of the following are true, use steps:**

### Scenario
Describing a workflow, troubleshooting guidance, and/or instructions.

### Amount
When more than three distinct actions are presented  
**Note:** If less than three steps are needed use bullet points instead of numbers

### Sequence
Information is sequential. This means you can't change the order of the items without losing their meaning.  
**Note:** If information is not sequential, use bullet points instead.

## 🪜 Step Guidance

When writing a step, use the guidance below to write, edit, or prompt Goose.

| Tone | Structure | Format |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Technical, precise, instructional, and clear | Use plain, direct language<br>Avoid jargon<br>6-8th grade reading level<br>Present tense<br>Sentence punctuation, no exclamation marks | One action per step<br>Bold UI elements<br>Use numbers for sequential information<br>Start with a strong verb<br>If additional context is needed, separate it from the step itself |

## 🔍 Best Practices to Highlight

### Match system/UI references
- Use exact names of buttons, menu items, fields, system messages
- Bold specific selections

### Write in a sequence
- All steps are sequential, therefore we use numbers (not bullets) for workflows, troubleshooting, or instructions

### Isolate one action per step
- The numbered step should only include the actual step an advocate needs to take, not the context that is considered
- If a decision is present in the step series, first consider if a new set of steps should be drafted. However, if the decision needs to take place within one series of steps, nest it under the action it's related to

**Example:**
1. Check for unauthorized transactions.
   - If unauthorized transactions are found, help the customer open a dispute claim. If not, continue with the steps below.

### Choose strong action verbs
Be as specific as possible without getting too technical.

✅ **Close**  
❌ **Terminate**

## 🔗 Examples

### ❌ Non-example of internal steps

**Create a new CF1 case**

If the customer's account was verified successfully and there are no duplicate cases, create a new CF1 case.

1. With the Sprinklr case open, select the Case tab.
2. Select the Edit icon.
3. Navigate to SFDC and paste the customer's C-Token and enter their email.
4. Under Description, insert the Social Escalation snippet from Text Blaze, and your notes, and then select Save.
5. Compose a message to the customer letting them know a case has been created and that they can expect a follow-up over email.

   **EXTREMELY IMPORTANT:** You must select Send when sending the message. This keeps the case in your queue allowing you to complete the CF1 case creation process.

6. Select Macro, followed by Create CF1 Case.
7. Select Apply from the new box that appears.
8. A CF1 case is created once it leaves your queue.

### ✅ Example of internal steps

**Create a new CF1 case**

If the customer's account is verified and there are no duplicate cases, create a new CF1 case using the steps below:

1. Open the **Case** tab in Sprinklr.
2. Click the **Edit** icon.
3. Navigate to SFDC and enter these customer details:
   - Customer C-Token
   - Customer email address
4. Add case information in the **Description** field:
   - Insert the Social Escalation snippet using Text Blaze.
   - Add your case notes.
5. Click **Save**.
6. Write a message to the customer with these details:
   - Confirm their case has been created.
   - Let them know to expect an email follow-up.
7. Click **Send**.
   - Clicking Send keeps the case in your queue so you can complete the process.
8. Click **Macro**.
9. Select **Create CF1 Case**.
10. Click **Apply** in the new window.
    - The case will leave your queue when the CF1 case is successfully created.

### Lists: There are two types of lists we use: ordered and unordered.

- **Ordered or numbered lists** are step-based or sequential. You can't change the order of the items without losing their meaning. Use this type of list for workflows, troubleshooting steps, and instructions.
- **Unordered or bulleted lists** are not step-based or sequential. Moving the order of the items will not change the meaning of the list. Each bullet should be parallel in form, such as all sentences or all noun phrases.

Don't include assumed "foundational" steps when providing instructions, such as 'turn on your phone, open the app.'
