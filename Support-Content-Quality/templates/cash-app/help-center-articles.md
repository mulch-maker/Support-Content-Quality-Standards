# Cash App Web Help Article Template Guide

*Source: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1n7jBoKVUVLarDNfocejxhIq2P_fFeaBBNjZiBpoPYdo/edit*

## Overview

The info and templates in this guide are intended to support your process as you scope, and provide consistency in the way we format and write articles for logged out Web.

### Goals:
- Provide a straightforward solution to a customer's issue so they can self serve
- Provide general guidance or information about using a feature, a process, or troubleshooting

### Best practices:
- Scope and draft at the same time as the rest of your help content (Quick Text and In-App) 
- Determine who your audience is and think about the type of content you are writing. Are you trying to educate the customer on a topic vs. provide steps to an issue they can solve? 

### Web vs. In-App: 
- **Web:** is logged out, and you are writing for a general audience. 
- **In-App:** Consider things like screen size, or mobile browser when determining length.
- Naming conventions may differ. Example: Web Version - How to Reset your PIN and In App Version - Reset PIN In-App

### Length of Content:
If you think your article may reach a limit of 4,500 CHARS, recommend breaking out your article into multiple articles.

### Media: 
Determine when scoping if a gif or video may be needed for your article. 

Articles that may benefit from gifs and or video:
- **How to:** A gif is a good visual to leverage to show step by step instructions on how to solve an issue in the app. 
- **Informational and Educational:** Evaluate if a video may help assist with providing long form content in a more condensed way that is easily digestible for a customer. 

### SEO: 
**Summary, Meta Title, and Description fields**
- **Summary:** Brief Description of the Article
- **Meta Title Tag:** The title tag is meant to accurately describe the content of the page and Google may display it on SERPs as a clickable headline while displaying the results. (Usually matches the title)
- **Meta Description:** This section is what drives the preview of the description of an article for internal search. (Usually matches the Summary)

For now, please keep all descriptions for these sections the same/similar. 

Refer to our [SEO guide](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1hlaP-ntBMXrYNu6HO-TVUdUlFak2RA2p2oJLA462fjQ/edit#heading=h.mrisx7u3a8y0) for best practices on writing for SEO. (Updates to this Guide Coming Soon)

We use sentence case in Help Center article body copy and section titles. We use title case in Help Center article titles.
